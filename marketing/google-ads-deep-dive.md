## 1. Google Ads Best Practices for Pool Builders

### Campaign Types — What Works Best

Pool companies should run a **layered campaign stack**. Here's each type ranked by priority:

#### Tier 1: Google Local Service Ads (LSA) — MUST HAVE
- **Pay per lead**, not per click — you only pay when someone contacts you
- Appears at the very top of Google (above regular ads)
- Shows the "Google Guaranteed" badge = instant trust
- Average cost per lead: **$20–$85** for home services; pool construction leads typically run **$50–$150/lead**
- **Start here** — this is the highest-ROI channel for local service businesses

#### Tier 2: Search Campaigns — PRIMARY WORKHORSE
- Text ads that appear when someone searches specific keywords
- Highest intent traffic — people actively searching for pool services
- Average CPC for pool keywords in Texas: **$5–$25** (construction keywords higher, service keywords lower)
- Full control over keywords, bids, ad copy, and landing pages
- **Create separate campaigns for each service type:**
  - New Pool Construction (highest value, highest CPC)
  - Pool Service & Maintenance (lower CPC, recurring revenue)
  - Repairs & Equipment (mid-range CPC, urgent intent)
  - Pool Remodeling/Renovation (high value)
  - Commercial Pool Services (separate targeting)

#### Tier 3: Performance Max — SUPPLEMENT (Add after Search is profitable)
- AI-driven campaign that runs across Search, Maps, YouTube, Gmail, Display
- Provide Google with images, text, videos, and audience signals
- Best used alongside a well-performing Search campaign
- Less control and visibility into what's working
- **Don't start here** — add after 60-90 days of Search data

#### Tier 4: Display Remarketing — RETARGETING ONLY
- Show banner ads to people who already visited your website
- Very low CPC ($0.50–$2.00) but lower intent
- Great for staying top-of-mind during the long pool buying cycle (60-120 days)
- **Only use for remarketing**, never for cold prospecting

#### What to AVOID
- **Broad Display campaigns** (cold targeting) — waste of budget for local services
- **YouTube ads** (unless you have a strong video library) — brand awareness, not leads
- **Discovery/Demand Gen campaigns** — better for e-commerce, not local services

### Call Tracking Setup — Why It Matters

**Phone calls are your #1 conversion type.** Over 60% of pool company leads come via phone call, not form submissions.

#### Recommended Setup: CallRail ($45-$145/month)
1. **Dynamic Number Insertion (DNI):** Swaps your phone number on the website with a tracking number based on how the visitor arrived (Google Ads, organic, direct, etc.)
2. **Dedicated tracking numbers:** Unique numbers for each ad campaign so you know which campaign generated the call
3. **Call recording:** Record every call for quality and training
4. **Google Ads integration:** Automatically reports calls as conversions in Google Ads, feeding the algorithm data to optimize
5. **Keyword-level tracking:** Know which exact keyword triggered the call

#### Setup Steps:
1. Sign up for CallRail (callrail.com)
2. Create a "Website Pool" of 4-6 tracking numbers
3. Install the CallRail JavaScript snippet on your website
4. Connect CallRail to Google Ads (Settings → Integrations → Google Ads)
5. Set minimum call duration for conversion (recommend **60 seconds** — filters out wrong numbers and tire-kickers)
6. Enable call recording
7. Create campaign-level tracking numbers for each Google Ads campaign

**Why this matters:** Without call tracking, you're flying blind. You won't know which keywords, ads, or campaigns are generating real phone calls. Google's algorithm also can't optimize for calls if it doesn't know they're happening.

### Quality Score Optimization

Quality Score is Google's 1-10 rating of your ad+keyword+landing page combo. Higher Quality Score = **lower CPC and better ad positions.**

The three components:

#### 1. Expected Click-Through Rate (CTR)
- Use the keyword in your headline (exact or close match)
- Write compelling ad copy with a clear benefit and CTA
- Use all ad extensions (adds visual space and improves CTR)
- Target: **5%+ CTR** for branded terms, **3%+ CTR** for non-branded

#### 2. Ad Relevance
- **Tight ad groups:** 5-10 closely related keywords per ad group
- Match keyword themes to ad copy themes
- Don't put "pool construction" and "pool cleaning" in the same ad group
- Use Responsive Search Ads with 15 headlines and 4 descriptions (pin the most important ones)

#### 3. Landing Page Experience
- Page must be relevant to the keyword AND the ad
- Fast load time (< 3 seconds)
- Mobile-friendly design
- Original, useful content (not just a form)
- Easy navigation
- Transparent business info (address, phone, license number)

**Quick Win:** Create dedicated landing pages per service. This alone can move Quality Score from 5/10 to 7-8/10, saving you **15-30% on CPC.**

### Keyword Match Types — When to Use Each

#### Exact Match [keyword]
- **When:** You know exactly what converts and want maximum control
- **Example:** [pool builder san antonio]
- **Use for:** Your top 10-20 highest-value keywords
- **Pros:** Most relevant traffic, highest conversion rates
- **Cons:** Limited volume

#### Phrase Match "keyword"
- **When:** You want your keyword plus close variations and additional words
- **Example:** "pool builder san antonio" → matches "best pool builder san antonio," "pool builder san antonio cost"
- **Use for:** Most of your keywords — best balance of reach and relevance
- **Pros:** Good balance of volume and relevance
- **Cons:** Can still match some irrelevant queries

#### Broad Match keyword
- **When:** You have strong conversion data (50+ conversions/month) AND Smart Bidding is active
- **Example:** pool builder san antonio → might match "someone to build a swimming pool in my backyard"
- **Use for:** Scaling after you've proven what works
- **Pros:** Maximum reach, discovers new search terms
- **Cons:** Can waste budget on irrelevant searches if not managed

**Recommended Approach:**
1. **Start with Phrase Match** for most keywords
2. **Add Exact Match** for your top 5-10 converting keywords (bid higher on these)
3. **Only add Broad Match** after 90+ days of data and with Smart Bidding enabled
4. **Always layer with robust negative keywords** regardless of match type

## 3. Geographic Targeting Deep Dive

### How to Target by Location in Google Ads

#### Option 1: Radius Targeting
- Set a radius around your office address (e.g., 30 miles)
- Good for starting out but not precise enough for optimization
- **Use as your base** then add bid adjustments by area

#### Option 2: Zip Code Targeting (RECOMMENDED)
- Target specific zip codes where your ideal customers live
- Allows bid adjustments per zip code (bid more in wealthy areas)
- **This is the most powerful approach for pool builders**

#### Option 3: City/Neighborhood Targeting
- Target named cities: San Antonio, Boerne, New Braunfels, etc.
- Can layer with radius for precision
- Good for smaller surrounding cities

### How to Set Up Zip Code Targeting:
1. Go to **Campaign Settings → Locations**
2. Click **"Enter another location"**
3. Type each zip code individually
4. Set bid adjustments per zip (e.g., +30% for 78257, -20% for lower-income areas)
5. Click **Advanced search** to bulk-add zip codes

### How to EXCLUDE Areas (Be a "Ghost")

To avoid showing ads in areas you don't want to serve:

1. Go to **Campaign Settings → Locations → Exclude**
2. Enter zip codes, cities, or neighborhoods to block
3. Your ads will NOT appear to people in those areas

**Why exclude?**
- Low-income areas unlikely to buy $80K+ pools
- Areas too far from your crews (drive time kills margins)
- Areas with soil/terrain issues that increase costs
- Competitor strongholds where you'd overpay for clicks

### ⚠️ CRITICAL SETTING: "Presence" vs "Presence or Interest"

**This is the #1 setting most agencies get wrong. Check Teckfactor's setup immediately.**

Google defaults to **"Presence or interest"** — this means your ads can show to someone in NEW YORK who googled "pool builder san antonio" (maybe researching for a move, or just browsing). They're not in your service area.

**CHANGE THIS to "Presence: People in or regularly in your targeted locations"**

How to change:
1. Campaign Settings → Locations
2. Click **"Location options"**
3. Target: Change to **"Presence: People in or regularly in your targeted locations"**
4. Exclude: Change to **"Presence: People in your excluded locations"**

**This one change alone can save 10-25% of wasted budget.**

### How to Use Bid Adjustments by Location

In Google Ads:
1. Go to **Locations** tab in your campaign
2. See performance data by location
3. Click the bid adjustment column
4. Enter percentage adjustments:

| Scenario | Bid Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Premium zip codes (Dominion, Stone Oak, Boerne) | **+25% to +40%** |
| High-value zip codes | **+10% to +20%** |
| Baseline areas | **0% (no adjustment)** |
| Low-priority but serviceable areas | **-20% to -50%** |
| Areas to avoid | **-100% (excludes entirely)** |

**Example:** If your base CPC bid is $15 and you have a +30% adjustment on 78257 (The Dominion), you'll bid up to $19.50 for clicks from that zip code, outbidding competitors for your most valuable audience.

## 4. Google Certifications & Trust Signals

### Google Guaranteed Badge (via Local Service Ads)

**What it is:** A green checkmark badge that says "Google Guaranteed" below your ad on Local Service Ads. Google backs your work with up to $2,000 in coverage if the customer isn't satisfied.

**How to qualify for Pool Builders:**
1. **Business license verification** — active state contractor license (Texas requires ONLY a general business license for pool builders, but verify current TDLR requirements)
2. **Insurance verification** — General liability insurance (minimum $500K, recommend $1M+)
3. **Background checks** — Owner and all field employees must pass background checks through Google's partner (Evident or Pinkerton)
4. **Google Business Profile** — Must be active and verified
5. **Minimum review rating** — Google prefers 3.0+ stars (aim for 4.5+)

**Cost:** No upfront fee. You only pay per lead (see LSA section). Google charges a small ongoing verification fee (~$50/month in some markets).

**Timeline to get approved:** 2-6 weeks

**Impact on business:**
- **Up to 13.8% higher click-through rate** compared to standard ads (Google's own data)
- Dramatically increases trust — homeowners feel protected
- Puts you above ALL regular Google Ads in search results

### Google Screened vs Google Guaranteed

| Feature | Google Guaranteed | Google Screened |
|---|---|---|
| **Who it's for** | Home services (pool builders, plumbers, etc.) | Professional services (lawyers, accountants) |
| **Badge** | Green checkmark | Blue checkmark |
| **Customer protection** | Up to $2,000 money-back guarantee | No money-back guarantee |
| **Requirements** | License + insurance + background check | License + background check |
| **For Worthey?** | **YES — get this** | Not applicable |

## 6. Google Local Service Ads (LSA) — Detailed Guide

### How LSA Works for Pool Builders

**LSA is fundamentally different from regular Google Ads:**

| Feature | Regular Google Ads | Local Service Ads |
|---|---|---|
| **Payment model** | Pay per click (whether they call or not) | Pay per lead (only when contacted) |
| **Position** | Below LSA results | TOP of Google (position zero) |
| **Trust badge** | None (unless manually added) | Google Guaranteed ✓ |
| **What shows** | Text ad + extensions | Photo + reviews + phone # + badge |
| **Targeting** | Keywords + location | Service category + location only |
| **Landing page** | Your website | Google's LSA profile page |
| **Control** | Full control over everything | Limited — Google decides a lot |

### How to Set Up and Get Approved

**Step-by-step:**

1. **Go to** ads.google.com/local-services-ads
2. **Create your profile:**
   - Business name: Worthey Aquatics
   - Service categories: Pool Contractor, Pool Cleaner, Pool Service (select all that apply)
   - Service areas: Enter your zip codes or radius
   - Business hours
   - Photos (min 3 — completed pool projects)
3. **Verification process:**
   - Upload business license/registration
   - Upload general liability insurance certificate
   - Upload workers' comp (if applicable, required in TX for 3+ employees)
   - Submit owner + employee background check authorization
   - Link Google Business Profile
4. **Set your budget:**
   - Choose weekly budget based on desired lead volume
   - Start with $500-$1,000/week to test
5. **Go live:** Typically 2-6 weeks after submitting all documents

### Average Cost Per Lead — LSA vs Search Ads

| Metric | Local Service Ads | Search Ads |
|---|---|---|
| **Cost model** | Per lead | Per click |
| **Pool construction lead** | $50–$150 per lead | $150–$400 per lead (at $15 CPC × 10-25 clicks per conversion) |
| **Pool service lead** | $20–$60 per lead | $40–$120 per lead (at $6 CPC × 7-20 clicks per conversion) |
| **Pool repair lead** | $30–$80 per lead | $60–$200 per lead |
| **Lead quality** | Phone call or message (direct contact) | Website visit (may or may not convert) |

**LSA is typically 30-60% cheaper per lead than traditional Search Ads** because you only pay for actual contacts, not clicks.

### How to Rank Higher in LSA

LSA doesn't use keywords or bids in the traditional sense. Here's what determines your ranking:

1. **Review count & rating (BIGGEST factor)**
   - More 5-star Google reviews = higher ranking
   - **Target: 50+ reviews with 4.8+ average within first 3 months**
   - Ask every happy customer for a review (text them a direct link)
   - Respond to EVERY review (positive and negative)

2. **Response time**
   - Answer calls and messages within **minutes**, not hours
   - Google tracks your responsiveness
   - Set up the LSA app on your phone for instant notifications
   - **Missed calls destroy your ranking**

3. **Proximity to searcher**
   - Google favors businesses closer to the person searching
   - Your office at 6002 Camp Bullis gives you an advantage in the 78257/78258 area

4. **Business hours**
   - Set extended hours if possible (7am-8pm captures more leads)
   - Weekend availability is a big plus

5. **Budget**
   - Higher weekly budgets get more exposure
   - But Google also factors in how well you handle leads

6. **Profile completeness**
   - All photos filled in (upload your best 10 pool photos)
   - All service categories selected
   - Complete business description
   - Booking/scheduling enabled if available

### LSA Lead Dispute Process

**You can dispute and get refunds for bad leads.** This is one of LSA's best features.

**Disputable leads:**
- Spam or robocalls
- Wrong service (someone asking for something you don't offer)
- Customer outside your service area
- Solicitation calls (vendors trying to sell you something)
- Job seekers
- Existing customers (already in your system)

**How to dispute:**
1. Open the Local Services app or go to the LSA dashboard
2. Find the lead in your lead list
3. Click "Dispute" and select the reason
4. Google reviews within 1-2 business days
5. If approved, the lead charge is reversed

**Dispute within 30 days** of the lead. Google allows disputes up to 30 days after the lead was received.

**Pro tip:** Dispute aggressively — many businesses leave money on the table by not disputing bad leads. Track every lead and dispute anything that's clearly not qualified.

### Remarketing / Retargeting Setup

**Remarketing shows ads to people who already visited your website. This is essential for pool builders because the buying cycle is 60-120 days.**

#### Setup Steps:
1. **Install Google Ads remarketing tag** on all pages of your website
2. **Create audience lists:**
   - All website visitors (last 90 days)
   - Visited construction page but didn't convert (last 60 days)
   - Visited service page but didn't convert (last 30 days)
   - Visited contact/quote page but didn't submit (last 14 days — these are HOT)
   - Past converters (submitted form or called — exclude from prospecting, target for upsell)
3. **Create a Display Remarketing campaign:**
   - Budget: 5% of total ad spend
   - Bid: $0.50-$2.00 CPC
   - Creative: Beautiful pool images + "Still dreaming? Let's make it happen" messaging
   - Frequency cap: Max 3 impressions per person per day (don't be creepy)
4. **Create an RLSA (Remarketing Lists for Search Ads):**
   - Add your remarketing audiences to Search campaigns as "Observation"
   - Bid +25% for past website visitors who search again
   - These people are MUCH more likely to convert
